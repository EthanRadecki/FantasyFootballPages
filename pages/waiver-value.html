Waiver Value

Position-adjusted value produced per week rostered, for every waiver and free agent pickup (2020-2025).

Methodology

The league used FAAB bidding only in 2025. From 2020-2024 it ran on reverse-order waiver priority, where a successful claim sends you to the back of the line. Since "cost" isn't comparable across those two systems, this page measures pure output instead.

Raw points aren't fair across positions though, a mediocre QB pickup outscores a great TE pickup every week just because QBs score more. So every pickup gets a position-adjusted value: how many standard deviations above or below the average rostered player at that position, that week, across the whole league. A value of 0 means "exactly average for a rostered player at that position." Most pickups score below zero, since most waiver adds are below-average by definition, that's expected, not a bug.

A stint ends at the next drop or trade-away, whichever comes first. Bye weeks are kept (real production, even without a fantasy opponent that week), but consolation/loser-bracket weeks are excluded, since teams are often already eliminated and not managed seriously by that point. Thomas Sullivan and William Serafin (2020 only) are excluded too.

Career Leaderboard
Points per week rostered, ranked by position-adjusted value.

Bars show points per week rostered (PPW), the intuitive number. Managers are ranked by position-adjusted value (z-score vs. other rostered players at that position), so bar order and bar length won't always match, that's the point. The axis and color scale rescale to fit each position (QB and K don't share a range), but stay fixed across seasons so year-to-year comparisons at the same position are fair. Hover a bar for the full breakdown, including that manager's best pickup in this view.
