Trade Value

Every real trade in league history (2020-2025), graded on four metrics and combined into one composite score.

Methodology

Value is based on actual production after the trade, using the same position-adjusted z-score approach as Waiver Value, not preseason rankings. Trade Grade is the raw value swing, what you got minus what you gave. Realized Gains is the same thing but only counts weeks the player was actually started, so a great acquisition that sat on your bench doesn't get full credit. Fit Score checks whether the trade actually upgraded a real roster weakness. Necessity Score compares the acquired player against the best alternative that was already sitting on your bench, unstarted, that same week, so a "need" only counts if there genuinely wasn't a better in-house option.

These four combine into QUAD, Quad-metric Unified Assessment of Deal, weighted 35/30/20/15 toward Realized Gains, Trade Grade, Fit, and Necessity respectively, then z-scored across every real trade. Positive QUAD means an above-average trade for this league; negative means below-average.

Trades that were made and then fully reversed (the ones done in anger or as a joke, then undone by league consensus) are excluded entirely. Multi-team trades get linked together and graded on each manager's true net position, not each 2-team leg in isolation, so a manager who briefly held a player only to flip him seconds later doesn't get penalized for value they never intended to keep. Thomas Sullivan and William Serafin (2020 only) are excluded, same as the rest of the site.

Trade Volume vs. Win Percentage
Does trading more actually correlate with winning? Bubble color shows whether a manager's trades tend to help or hurt.

Each bubble is one manager, positioned by trades made (x-axis) and career win percentage (y-axis, bracket-only games, consolation weeks excluded). Green bubbles have historically made good trades on average (positive QUAD); red bubbles haven't. Across this league the relationship is weak, trading a lot doesn't reliably predict winning more, some of the most active traders sit below .500 and some of the least active sit above it.
