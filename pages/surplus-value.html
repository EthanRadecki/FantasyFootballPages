Surplus Value Index

How much value did each manager extract from every pick, relative to what that slot should produce? (2020-2024)

How It Works

Surplus Value measures how much a drafted player over- or under-performed relative to the historical average for their draft slot and position. For each pick, we find all players taken within 3 picks of that slot at the same position across all seasons and compute their average PPR/game. That becomes the expected value. Subtract expected from actual and you get the surplus. A round 9 RB who scores 18 PPG when the average round-9 RB scores 10 PPG has a surplus of +8. A round 1 WR who gets injured and scores 3 PPG when the average is 16 PPG has a surplus of -13. Aggregate every pick for a manager and you get their draft grade.
