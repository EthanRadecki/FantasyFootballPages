Surplus Value Index

How much value did each manager extract from every pick, relative to what that slot should produce? (2020-2024)

How It Works

For each drafted player, we find all players taken within 3 picks of that spot at the same position across all seasons and compute their average PPR/game. That's the expected value. Subtract expected from actual and you get the surplus. Stack all surpluses for a manager and you get their draft grade. Picks where the player appeared in fewer than 8 games count as 0 surplus, not negative, since injuries are random and shouldn't tank a manager's career grade. Stats are sourced directly from ESPN fantasy scoring to match your exact league settings.

Note on Best Picks

Surplus is calculated against players drafted within 3 spots at the same position across all seasons, so a round 14 RB is compared to other round 14 RBs, not to all RBs. This makes late-round steals look huge and early picks look modest, since expectations are already high early. Minimum 8 games played to qualify.

Note on Busts

Busts are limited to rounds 1-7 since late-round misses are expected and don't tell you much. Picks with fewer than 8 games played count as 0 surplus, so injury-related busts like Aaron Rodgers 2023 (1 game played) don't appear here. These are the picks where a manager had a healthy player who just didn't perform.
