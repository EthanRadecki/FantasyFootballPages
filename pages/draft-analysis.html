How It Works

Dominance Score measures how much better or worse a team scored compared to the league average each week. Manager-Adjusted Performance compares how a manager actually performed from each draft slot versus what we would expect based on their career average. A positive value means that slot tends to produce better results than the manager's baseline.

Methodology

A pick is a hit if the player finished top 24 at WR or RB, or top 7 at QB or TE, in PPR points per game for that season. Thresholds are calibrated to the 14-team league size. Stats are sourced directly from ESPN fantasy scoring to match your exact league settings. Data covers all six seasons, 2020-2025. Players with fewer than 8 games are excluded from hit rate calculations, since small samples are not reliable indicators of talent.

Reading the Gradient

Bar colors use an absolute scale where 0% is always red and 100% is always green, with bone at 50%. This means you can compare across all three charts. A medium-green early TE (100%) looks very different from a dark-red late WR (7.9%), even though both might look "good" or "bad" on a relative scale. The gradient reflects the actual hit rate, not just how each position compares within its tier.

How Steals Are Ranked

Picks are ranked by points above position average, not raw PPR/game, so positions are comparable. A WR who scores 8 points above the average WR beats a QB who scores 5 points above the average QB, even if the QB's raw number is higher. Minimum 8 games played to qualify. Rounds 8 and later only.

What is Surplus Value?

Every pick has an expected value based on what players taken at that slot and position have historically produced. Surplus value is the difference between what you actually got and what you should have expected. A round 14 RB who scores 20 PPG when round-14 RBs average 8 PPG is a +12 surplus. Picks where the player appeared in fewer than 8 games count as 0, not negative, since injuries are random. Carmine Pittelli leads the league all-time at +0.163 per pick. Ryan McQuaid is last at -0.305.
