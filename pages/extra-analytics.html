Schedule Luck
Who has the schedule been kind to?
How It Works

Each week, every manager's score is compared to the league median. If you scored above the median, you "should have" won that week regardless of your actual opponent. The difference between your actual wins and these expected wins is your schedule luck. Positive means the schedule gifted you wins; negative means it took them away.

Seasonal Analysis
Which quarter of the season matters most?
Methodology

A logistic regression tested which part of the season best predicts playoff qualification, using average points scored each quarter as independent variables. The model achieved an AUC of 0.83, indicating strong predictive accuracy. Q2 (Weeks 4-6) and Q4 (Weeks 10-13) carry the largest coefficients, while Q3 (Weeks 7-9) is the weakest predictor.

Across six seasons the two conferences are basically identical. Same average scoring, same average win rate, same number of playoff trips. REP has four titles to DEM's two, but that's a coin flip landing one way six times, not a real edge. The schedule doesn't even favor same-conference matchups: only 46% of all-time games have come within a conference, barely different from the 50% you'd get by chance. DEM and REP are a fun label, not a competitive divide.

Championship Gauntlet
Who had to earn it?
How It Works

Each title run is scored on three things: how many points opponents actually scored against the champion, how strong those opponents were across the regular season, and whether they were hot or cold in the five games right before facing the champion. Every score is measured against the full history of the league, not just these six runs, so the scale stays fixed as new champions get added. Runs with fewer playoff games get a small statistical adjustment so a three-game path isn't judged unevenly against a four-game path. Weighted 40/30/30 and mapped to a fixed 0-100 scale every year.

Gauntlet Score blends three z-scored components: points scored against the champion, opponent regular-season dominance, and opponent hot streak entering the game. Each is measured against the full league history and mapped through a fixed curve, not scaled to just these six runs. Games are lightly shrunk toward the league average based on how many rounds each champion played, so three-game and four-game runs compare fairly. Weighted 40/30/30. A score near 50 is a typical path. Anything further out means the field was tougher or softer than usual.
